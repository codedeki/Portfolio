About me

I'm a self-taught full-stack developer who has a strong understanding of computer science despite not coming from the STEM field. I have one year professional experience with a combined five years of coding experience. I think having a degree in computer science does not make one a developer any more than reading books and listening to lectures about fixing cars makes one a mechanic. Although books are useful and I read practical theory to improve my understanding, I think the best developer is the one who builds applications, not the one who studies or who has a PhD. In my experience, 60% of the developer's job is knowing how to do research when you don't have the solution to a problem - a skill that my BA in History and English Literature taught me well. The other 40% involves planning, organizing, problem-solving and, lastly, the coding. Following my undergraduate studies, I spent five years working as a writing tutor for the Writing Support Center at the University of Western Ontario, where I taught research and writing skills to a great many students. If you're wondering about my soft skills, you should know that by majority student opinion I was voted as "the most approachable writing tutor" from among a dozen of my colleagues. I attribute my ability to build web apps by myself to my advanced research and problem-solving skills which help me learn as I go and navigate third-party APIs. I also have a dog who I'm crazy about and play chess in my spare time.
